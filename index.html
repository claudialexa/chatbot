When a storm affects your area, the first thing you want to know is where to go and what to avoid.

AfterStorm allows you to do this by showing you flooded routes, finding businesses that are open near you and giving you access to other important emergency relief information.

Behind the App

Inspiration

Using natural language processing and machine learning we created an application that provides you with the information citizens most want and need during a weather disaster, hopefully easing some of the anxiety that they are experiencing and connecting them to important safety information and government services.

Motivation

After speaking to residents and hearing their concerns, we knew we wanted to make something that would make their lives before, during, and after a flood easier. We created Iris, a chatbot that helps connect people to information and government services, think of it as customer service for natural disasters.

Execution

Iris is built using Facebook's AI and Natural Language Processing technologies. It's designed to evolve over time and connect to more services and databases as the situation changes. Iris is accessible through Facebook or directly through the Afterstorm App.

Features

Intuitive Interface

Afterstorm is a messaging app that makes it easier to contact essential services in a time of emergency. It uses the same interface as other well known messaging applications that is already known by millions of users. Simply login and start asking questions.

Integrated databases

Iris is intuitive and agregates information from multiple databases to provide you with context aware services. As datasources are added to the system, Iris learns and provides better answerss

Pre-emptive Alerts

Iris sends you alerts on your phone before, during, and after a storm to keep you updated with important weather and safety information. Alerts are more than just weather, they also include important information from city officials, notifications you subscribed to (for example from your power company) and local news headlines from a recent storm event. If you want Iris to keep watch on your local grocery store opens, she can also do that and send you an alert when they re-open.

A Few Use Cases

Trying to Avoid a Flooded Route

One of the biggest concerns for residents when a flood occurs is getting back home when streets begin to get flooded. Due to bad drainage, many roads can get so flooded that many cars can’t make it out. AfterStorm makes avoiding these streets easy with voice command: you can ask it to find flooded areas or to take you home avoiding flooded streets.

Finding the Nearest Open Gas Station

After a weather emergency there are few apps that will tell you actual operating hours of businesses near you. This often means either searching the web, calling around, or avoiding going out to shops altogether. Unfortunately, when there is a weather emergency, there are certain trips that cannot be avoided, such as getting gas. Iris provides you with updated business hours and businesses near you that are open.

Reporting a Closed Business

AfterStorm’s information crowdsourcing feature makes it quick and easy to report opening hours for everything from a store to a shelter as well as potential risks during a flood: powerlines that are down, flood debris, water contamination, etc). AfterStorm was made for reporting, which can be done via voice or with just a few typed commands.
